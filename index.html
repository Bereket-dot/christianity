The Tree of Knowledge

"And the Lord God commanded the man, saying, 'You may surely eat of every tree of the garden, but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat, for in the day that you eat of it you shall surely die.'"

Genesis 2:16-17 reveals the divine commandment that tested humanity's obedience. This pivotal moment in sacred history represents humanity's choice between trusting God's wisdom or seeking autonomous knowledge.

The Apostles of Christ

Foundations of the Early Church

Peter

Originally named Simon, Jesus renamed him Peter (meaning "rock"). He was a fisherman and became the leader of the apostles.

Key Contributions: Preached at Pentecost, wrote two epistles, traditionally martyred in Rome.

Scriptures: Matthew 16:18, Acts 2, 1 & 2 Peter

Read More

John

The "beloved disciple," brother of James. He wrote the Gospel of John, three epistles, and Revelation.

Key Contributions: Cared for Mary, emphasized love, exiled to Patmos.

Scriptures: Gospel of John, 1-3 John, Revelation

Read More

Paul

Originally Saul, persecutor of Christians, converted on the road to Damascus. Apostle to the Gentiles.

Key Contributions: Wrote 13 epistles, established churches throughout Asia Minor and Europe.

Scriptures: Acts 9, Romans, Corinthians, Galatians, etc.

Read More

James

Brother of John, one of the "Sons of Thunder." First apostle martyred (Acts 12:2).

Key Contributions: Part of Jesus' inner circle, present at Transfiguration.

Scriptures: Matthew 4:21, Mark 3:17, Acts 12:2

Read More

Andrew

Peter's brother, originally a disciple of John the Baptist. Known for bringing people to Jesus.

Key Contributions: Brought Peter to Jesus, missionary to Scythia and Greece.

Scriptures: John 1:40-42, Matthew 4:18-20

Read More

Matthew

Former tax collector, author of the first Gospel. Also known as Levi.

Key Contributions: Wrote Gospel emphasizing Jesus as Messiah fulfilling prophecy.

Scriptures: Matthew 9:9, Gospel of Matthew

Read More
